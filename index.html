01

Research

Stellar Surface Mapping

Extending frameworks for stellar surface reconstruction using spherical harmonics. Astrometry accesses modes invisible to photometry—particularly odd-ℓ modes encoding hemispheric asymmetries.

Astrometric Jitter

Reframing "astrometric jitter" as a signal encoding stellar surface structure rather than noise. Quantifying stellar activity limits for missions like TOLIMAN.

Earth-twin Detection

High-precision astrometry offers a path to detecting Earth-mass planets in habitable zones—a parameter space largely inaccessible to Doppler spectroscopy.

Astrophotonics

Developing novel optical systems to extend exoplanet detection to lower masses and wider separations, particularly diffractive pupil techniques.

Wolf-Rayet Stars

These rare extreme stars constitute ~15% of radio stars. Interested in simultaneous optical and radio observations as probes of stellar magnetic physics.

SETI

Maintaining an interest in the role of high-precision astrometry in technosignature searches—astrometry isn't just for exoplanets.
